<p><p>Homojunction silicon photovoltaic cells are primarily classified into two market types: monocrystalline silicon and polysilicon. Monocrystalline silicon cells are made from a single crystal structure, offering higher efficiency and performance, particularly in limited space. In contrast, polysilicon cells are composed of multiple crystal structures, making them more cost-effective but generally less efficient. Both types cater to different market segments, with monocrystalline cells favored for residential and premium applications, while polysilicon cells are often used in larger, utility-scale installations.</p></p>
