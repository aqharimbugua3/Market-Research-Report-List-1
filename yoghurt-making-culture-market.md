<p><p>The yoghurt-making culture market is categorized into three primary types: mesophilic, thermophilic, and probiotics. Mesophilic cultures thrive at moderate temperatures, producing creamy yoghurt with a tangy flavor. Thermophilic cultures require higher temperatures, resulting in firmer, tangy yoghurt often used for commercial production. Probiotics, which are beneficial live bacteria, enhance health benefits and are increasingly sought after by health-conscious consumers. Each type caters to different consumer preferences and production methods, driving innovation and diversity in the yoghurt market.</p></p>
