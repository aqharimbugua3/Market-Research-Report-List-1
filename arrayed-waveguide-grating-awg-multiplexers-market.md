<p><p>The Arrayed Waveguide Grating (AWG) multiplexers market includes two primary types: thermal AWG and athermal AWG. Thermal AWGs are temperature-sensitive devices that require temperature stabilization to maintain performance, making them suitable for environments with controlled conditions. In contrast, athermal AWGs are designed to operate across a wider temperature range without significant performance variations, enhancing stability and reliability. This robustness makes athermal AWGs particularly appealing for telecom applications, where fluctuating temperatures are common, promoting their adoption in various regions.</p></p>
